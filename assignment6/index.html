The Herald Sign

  • Top of the Building

The Herald sign is a stable of downtown Bellingham, illuminating the night sky as an icon to Bellingham locals. In 2016 during some renovations the building opted to replace their old, red illuminated sign with an RGB one to be able to change the colors for events and celebrations. For example in the image above the sign is in rainbow colors during the month of June to celebrate Pride Month.

Restaurants in the Space

Culture Cafe

Culture Cafe is a Hawaiian Restaurant located on the northwestern side of the Herald Building. They have an experimental kitchen, trying many different types of dishes that extend beyond typical Hawaiian food.

Cosmos Bistro

On the south end of the building next to the original entrance sits Cosmo Bistro, a relatively new addition to downtown established in 2015. They serve tradition American with am emphasis on food for those with allergies or specific diets.

Yelp Page

Rock and Rye Oyster House

Rock and Rye Oyster House sits on the South East corner of the building and serves Seafood and American food. They also have a bar where they sell craft cocktails, beer, cider, and wine.

Yelp Page

Quantum Leaf

Between Rock and Rye Oyster House and Cosmos Bistro sits Quantum Leaf, a vegan restaurant. While also being vegan they are also Gluten-Free and have an Alcohol-Free bar.

Architecture of the Harold

The Entrance

The entrance of the Harold Building features a Tudor arch decorated with gothic carvings. Above the arch sits terra cotta tiles which sits the lettering "Herald Building". On the archway sits a gate-like design that is followed through to the roofs design.

The Windows

The original windows on the base of the building have been replaced but the primary face features terra cotta cladding around the window frame with small inlays built around it. On the Inside it was also the first building in bellingham to have steam heating.

The Roofing

Sitting on top the roof sits the metal frame holding the famous Harold sign surrounded by a pillared design wrapping the building. The center of the southwest side sees an extension of the entrances gate like style.

History of the Harold

  • The Herald Building was built in 1926
  • Tallest building in Bellingham when originally built
  • First building in downtown Bellingham to have a concrete and steel form.
  • The building was built with a Gothic style, a style that was reborn with the book, Hunchback of Notre Dame, not long before.
  • Designed by Frederick Stanley Piper and architect firm Morrison & Stimson.
  • The building was built for the Bellingham Publishing Company which is now known as the Bellingham Herald
  • The building is listed in the National Register of Historic Places.
  • While they have moved out to the Barkley District, the building today houses businesses and local residents
